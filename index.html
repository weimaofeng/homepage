About EIRA Lab

At EIRA Lab, we investigate how people search when their goals are evolving, uncertain, multimodal, emotional, and shaped by context. Our work connects information retrieval with user information need, interaction design, and immersive access so that retrieval systems can respond not only to keywords, but also to intent, behaviour, and the lived experience of information seeking.

We build human-centered retrieval models and interfaces that support exploration, understanding, and decision-making across text, image, speech, and multimodal environments. By combining behavioural evidence, interaction signals, and theory-driven design, we aim to create retrieval systems that are more inclusive, adaptive, and meaningful for diverse users and real-world tasks.

What We Care About

User Information Need

Understanding how needs emerge, change, and become actionable during search.

Interactive Retrieval

Designing systems that support dialogue, exploration, feedback, and refinement.

Multimodal Access

Expanding search beyond text through image, speech, sketch, VR, and AR interaction.

Inclusive Experience

Building access methods that are usable, interpretable, and responsive to different people and settings.
